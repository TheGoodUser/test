💬 Essential HR Questions

1. "Tell me about yourself" (2 minutes)
✅ GOOD ANSWER:
"I'm a mobile developer with 15 months of professional experience. I started as the sole Android developer at a startup where I built applications and serverless infrastructure that helped secure 2 major clients. Recently, I've been expanding my skillset into full-stack development, learning Flutter, Docker, and Next.js. I'm excited about this internship because it aligns perfectly with my goal to become a well-rounded full-stack developer while contributing to real-world projects."
❌ BAD ANSWER:
"Umm, I'm from Bhilai. I studied engineering. I like coding. I need a job because I've been unemployed for 7 months."
💡 Pro Tip:
Structure: Current role → Key achievement → What you're learning → Why this role
2. "Why do you want this internship?"
✅ GOOD ANSWER:
"I'm looking to expand from Android-specific development into full-stack work. I've already started learning Flutter and backend technologies, but I want hands-on experience building complete applications from frontend to backend. Your company's focus on [mention something specific from job posting] really aligns with where I want to grow. Plus, as someone who's already handled infrastructure and architecture, I can contribute from day one while learning new technologies."
❌ BAD ANSWER:
"I need experience. Any internship is fine. I want to learn new things."
💡 Pro Tip:
Research the company. Mention specific projects/tech they use. Show you've done homework!
3. "What are your salary expectations?"
✅ GOOD ANSWER:
"Based on my 15 months of experience and expanded skillset including Docker, Next.js, and serverless architecture, I'm looking for ₹25,000 to ₹30,000 per month. I can handle full-stack responsibilities and contribute across frontend, backend, and DevOps, which I believe justifies this range."
❌ BAD ANSWER:
"Whatever you think is fair" OR "As much as possible" OR "₹50,000"
💡 Pro Tip:
Give a range (₹25-30k). Justify with skills. Be confident but flexible.
4. "Why did you leave your previous role?"
✅ GOOD ANSWER:
"I wanted to transition from Android-specific development to full-stack. I took time to upskill in Flutter, Docker, Next.js, and modern DevOps practices. Now I'm ready to apply these skills in a professional environment and continue growing."
❌ BAD ANSWER:
"The salary was too low" OR "I didn't like my boss" OR "I got laid off"
💡 Pro Tip:
Frame as: Learning opportunity + Career growth. Never badmouth previous employer!
5. "What's your biggest weakness?"
✅ GOOD ANSWER:
"I tend to dive deep into solving problems, which sometimes means I need to step back and consider the bigger picture. I'm working on this by setting regular check-ins during projects to ensure I'm aligned with overall goals, not just fixing immediate issues."
❌ BAD
